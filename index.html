    Speaking their language?: Multilingualism in party communication across democracies

    Our study examines how political parties navigate linguistic diversity in modern democracies. Naturally, multilingual political communication only emerges in settings where multiple languages are spoken.     Polarization but not populism strengthens the association between presidential election results and emotions

    We investigate whether election results are associated with emotional reactions among voters across democracies and under what conditions these responses are more intense. Building on recent work in comparative politics, we theorize that emotional intensity is stronger after elections involving populist candidates and highly polarized parties. We test these expectations with a big-data analysis of emotional reactions on partiesâ€™ Facebook posts during 29 presidential elections in 26 democracies. The results show that ideological polarization of political parties might intensify emotional reactions, but there is no clear relationship with the presence of populist candidates.
