We investigate whether election results are associated with emotional reactions among voters across democracies and under what conditions these responses are more intense. The results show that ideological polarization of political parties might intensify emotional reactions, but there is no clear relationship with the presence of populist candidates.

About the Project

The Global Political Social Media Project (GPS-POL) is an international research initiative dedicated to collecting, preserving, and sharing political social media data across democracies. Our goal is to support transparent, reproducible research on political behavior, communication, and democratic processes.

Our Team

GPS-POL is led by an interdisciplinary team of scholars in political science, psychology, and computational social science. The project is based at Washington University in St. Louis and collaborates with researchers worldwide.
